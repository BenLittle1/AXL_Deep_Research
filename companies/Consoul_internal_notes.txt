SECTION 1: COMPANY OVERVIEW


Company Information


Q: Company Name

A: Consoul



Concept Summary


Q: What problem are you solving? For who? What is the result?

A: We address the challenge faced by one in three employees who struggle daily to balance full-time work with complex at-home caregiving responsibilities. The invisible workload costs employers an estimated $300 billion per year in lost productivity, disengagement, and support costs.



Q: What is the industry or sector categorization?

A: Employee benefits SaaS within the HRTech and corporate wellness sector, focused specifically on digital caregiving support solutions.



Q: Is anyone else doing this? How are you different?

A: Key competitors include Wealthy, Caring Bridge, and Inana Care. Consoul differentiates through proprietary AI-driven personalization, deep integrations with HR systems, and an all-in-one mobile interface that delivers tailored care coordination and resources—capabilities others lack in combination.



Company Details


Q: Is this a founder-led idea, corporate initiative, or based on existing IP?

A: This is a founder-driven solution born from Cindy’s HR leadership and Stefanie’s finance expertise, validated by $400K in grant funding and proprietary IP protections secured to date.



Q: What have you accomplished to date (idea, concept, actively developing, scaling, etc.)?

A: We’ve raised $400K in grants, launched our MVP in August 2023, protected core IP, completed HIPAA/GDPR compliance, and are in beta with four pilot organizations alongside 100 paid subscriptions.



Q: Do you have any preliminary traction or purchase intent from existing or prospective customers?

A: Our four pilot partners—including Shoppers Drug Mart, Loblaw Companies, and Qualicare—have converted 100 subscriptions and provided strong testimonials on engagement uplifts, positioning us for enterprise rollouts.



Q: Are you Toronto-based or willing to move to Toronto to develop this idea?

A: Headquartered in Toronto, we’re deeply embedded in Canada’s innovation ecosystem and maintain strong ties with leading national retailers and healthcare institutions.





SECTION 2: BUSINESS PROPOSAL


Customer


Q: Who has this problem? What is the market size of the solution?

A: Our target market is HR and benefits decision-makers at mid-to-large enterprises globally—organizations with established wellness programs serving the $414.6 billion global caregiving economy, a $20 billion SAM, and a $2 billion obtainable market in employee support tools.



Q: Who is the ideal customer? Sector, size, problem, people, frequency.

A: Enterprises with 5,000+ employees in retail, healthcare, and finance, equipped with formal wellness budgets and looking for AI-driven benefits that boost productivity and employee engagement.



Q: Who is not the ideal customer? What do you run away from?

A: Small businesses under 500 employees without dedicated HR or benefits budgets, where our integrated platform’s value proposition cannot be fully leveraged.



Value Proposition


Q: Is there a quantifiable value proposition? What is it?

A: Consoul recaptures a share of the $300 billion annual productivity loss, translating to a projected $5 million ARR by 2027. Our go-to-market plan (slide 6) outlines paths to $3 million in direct sales and $2 million via partnerships and inbound channels, delivering measurable ROI on benefits spend.



Q: How often (frequency) does your user have the problem? How intense is the problem?

A: One in three employees experiences caregiving-related challenges daily, leading to persistent stress, disengagement, and support overhead for employers.



Q: How will they know if your product has solved the problem?

A: Customers track success through reductions in caregiving-related absenteeism, increases in employee engagement scores, decreased support ticket volumes, and ROI on benefits investments.



Feature Set


Q: What is the MVP? Do you have a feature development roadmap?

A: Our MVP, launched August 2023, delivers a mobile dashboard for AI-driven care coordination, personalized resource recommendations, and seamless HR system integration. Next steps include Azure Health Data Services integration and ORCHA certification.



Q: Who will use the first 'bad' product? Are they paying customers, partners, free users?

A: Our first adopters are HR teams at Shoppers Drug Mart, Loblaw Companies, and Baycrest, onboarding 100 subscriptions in our pilot and actively endorsing the platform.



Q: Does your product actually solve the problem? How and why not?

A: Pilot testimonials from Shoppers and Loblaw confirm problem-solution fit, highlighting immediate alleviation of caregiving stress and demonstrable productivity improvements.



Business Model


Q: Who is your early customer? How do you know them?

A: Our earliest customers are enterprise retail and healthcare organizations with high caregiver populations and existing wellness budgets—Shoppers, Loblaw, and Baycrest—offering the fastest path to expansion.



Q: What is your pricing model? How do you price your product?

A: We use a tiered subscription model averaging $10K ARR per client, with enterprise contracts, partnership bundles, and volume discounts aligned to ROI and organizational size.



Q: Who are your buyers? How do you know them?

A: Our buyers are HR and benefits executives who control corporate wellness budgets and seek solutions that drive measurable productivity gains and employee engagement.





SECTION 3: PARTNERSHIP WITH AXL


Why You?


Q: Why are you the best person to turn this idea into a billion-dollar company?

A: Co-founders Cindy Diogo (HR design & caregiving benefits) and Stefanie Kociuba (finance & grants management) bring complementary domain expertise, backed by CTO Karl Etherly’s technical leadership and Fabio H Buritica’s marketing acumen.



Q: What role do you see yourself playing at the company moving forward?

A: Cindy Diogo leads as CEO, guiding product vision and enterprise partnerships; Stefanie Kociuba oversees operations and finance as COO/CFO to scale sustainably.



Q: Who are your first 5 ideal hires?

A: Next hires: VP of Sales to drive enterprise outreach; Head of Customer Success to optimize onboarding and retention; Senior AI Engineer to advance personalization algorithms; Product Manager to lead feature roadmap; Marketing Lead to scale digital acquisition.



Why Us?


Q: What can AXL do to best support the development of your idea?

A: AXL’s network can accelerate enterprise sales through targeted introductions, offer mentorship on scaling B2B SaaS, and provide technical workshops to refine our AI capabilities and compliance integrations.



Q: How intensive do you believe your idea will be on AXL's technical team? Be as specific as possible.

A: We seek AXL’s cloud infrastructure credits, AI research support, and guidance on implementing HIPAA/GDPR-compliant data architectures to enhance our platform’s robustness and security.



Q: Are you looking for support on the business development and market validation process?

A: We request mentorship on enterprise sales strategies, partnership development best practices, and fundraising guidance to accelerate our go-to-market and global expansion in North America and Europe.



Why Now?


Q: You have lots of options. Why are you the right person to do this now?

A: With our MVP live, $400K in grants secured, pilots underway, and the $414.6 billion caregiving market primed for digital innovation, the time is right to scale Consoul.



Q: What has changed in the market to make this the right time to launch your solution?

A: Demographic shifts toward aging populations, growing dual-caregiver roles, and heightened employer focus on retention and digital benefits create an ideal window for Consoul’s launch.



Q: How will this idea stay ahead or stay differentiated long enough to become the market leader?

A: Our proprietary AI personalization engine, end-to-end HR integrations, and advanced compliance certifications (GDPR/HIPAA completed, ORCHA pending) establish high switching costs and long-term defensibility.
